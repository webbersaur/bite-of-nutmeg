The Dinner Rut

Be honest — how many restaurants do you actually go to? Three? Maybe four? It's not that you don't like trying new places. It's that when you're hungry and tired and someone asks "where do you want to eat?", your brain defaults to the same safe choices every time.

You know there are other options. You've been to them before. You liked them. You just... forgot they existed.

The Fix Is Simpler Than You Think

Just scroll through your town's restaurant list. That's it. No algorithm, no recommendations engine, no "based on your preferences." Just a complete list of every restaurant in your town.

The moment you see a name you recognize but haven't visited in months, something clicks. "Oh right, that place! We should go there." That's the memory jogger in action — not a fancy feature, just a list that reminds you of what you already know.
